Dr. Alan Turing

The man who played a crucial role in saving many lives during World War II is also considered the father of modern computer science, artificial intelligence, and cryptography.

Here's a time line of Dr. Alan Turing's life:

  • 1912: Alan Turing is born on June 23 in Maida Vale, London, England.
  • 1931: He studies mathematics at King's College, Cambridge.
  • 1936: Turing writes a famous paper about the "Turing Machine," a key idea in computer science.
  • 1938: Receives his Ph.D. from Princeton University, where he works on mathematical logic and cryptanalysis.
  • 1939: During World War II, Turing starts working at Bletchley Park, the UKâ€™s codebreaking center.
  • 1940: He helps to break the German Enigma code, which helps the Allies in the war.
  • 1942: Turing designs the Bombe, an electromechanical device used to decipher Enigma-encrypted messages, leading to numerous Allied victories.
  • 1945: Awarded the Order of the British Empire (OBE) for his wartime services.
  • 1945: Until 1948, Turing worked on designing a computer at the National Physical Laboratory.
  • 1948: He becomes a professor at the University of Manchester and continues his work on computers.
  • 1950: Turing writes a famous paper about artificial intelligence and the "Turing Test."
  • 1954: Alan Turing dies on June 7 at the age of 41, but he is remembered as one of the greatest thinkers in computer science.
